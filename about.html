Introduction

Covid-19 is a super infectious disease that has taken the world by storm. Information and resources pertaining to Covid-19 are constantly changing and developing. It is imperative for people to understand the risks of this disease, as well as how to protect others. The goal of Covid Hub is to provide a way for people to communicate and stay informed at the same time - an all in one hub. This is accomplished through up-to-date resources, a map that helps people track where Covid infected patients are, and a discussion board for people to talk about their experiences.

Inspiration

We are a group of 3 college students attending the University of North Carolina at Chapel Hill. Our first year of college was cut short due to the pandemic and we were unable to return to campus for our second year. Some of us also have friends who have tragically lost family members. We are tired of the poor handling by the government and mass ignorance online. Therefore, we created this site to educate more people on the risks their actions may bring. We included a discussion board in the hopes for people to talk about their experiences.

How This Site Works

We tried to implement this site in the most intuitive way possible. On the top right, you will find our navigation bar. Simply click on a word to navigate to that tab. Click the logo on the top left to reset the page. On the home page, you can chat with our bot to see if your following covid guidelines. On the discussion page, you will find our forum where you can post about anything. The map tab contains an interactive map that updates cases in real time. Fill out our relief form to recieve monetary assistance or advice. The useful links tab contains a compilation of helpful articles.
